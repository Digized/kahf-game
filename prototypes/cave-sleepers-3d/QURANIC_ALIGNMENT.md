# Quranic Alignment - Cave Sleepers 3D

This document maps each game scene to the corresponding verses in Surah Al-Kahf (18:9-26).

---

## Verse-by-Verse Mapping

### **18:9** - *"Do you think that the people of the cave and the inscription were among Our wonders a wonder?"*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Title screen**: Establishes the story as a divine wonder
- **Start scene**: Player becomes one of the youth, framing the story as extraordinary
- **Endings**: Reflection on whether becoming a "sign" is accepted or rejected

---

### **18:10** - *"When the youths retreated to the cave and said, 'Our Lord, grant us from Yourself mercy and prepare for us from our affair right guidance.'"*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `prayer_before_flight`**
  - Dedicated scene where believers pray before fleeing
  - Direct quote: *"Our Lord, grant us mercy from Yourself, and provide for us right guidance in our affair."*
  - Animation: Characters bow in prayer together
  - **Gap filled:** Original visual novel lacked this explicit prayer scene

---

### **18:11** - *"So We sealed their ears in the cave for a number of years."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `divine_sleep`**
  - Text: *"And God sealed their ears in the cave for years."* (direct reference)
  - Not ordinary sleep, but divine preservation
  - Animation: Characters lie down, environment fades
  - **Gap filled:** Original version didn't explicitly mention the sealing

---

### **18:12** - *"Then We awakened them that We might show which of the two factions was most precise in calculating what [extent] they had remained in time."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `confusion_about_time`**
  - Text: *"How long have we stayed?" They were disputing among themselves about their affair."* (18:21 reference)
  - Different believers guess different durations
  - God's knowledge emphasized over human calculation
  - **Gap filled:** Original version had vague time confusion, now it's a central debate

---

### **18:13** - *"It is We who relate to you, [O Muhammad], their story in truth. Indeed, they were youths who believed in their Lord, and We increased them in guidance."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `gathering`**
  - Establishes them as young believers
  - Faith as the core motivation for fleeing
  - Community of believers (not isolated individuals)

---

### **18:14** - *"And We strengthened their hearts when they stood up and said, 'Our Lord is the Lord of the heavens and the earth. Never will we invoke besides Him any deity.'"*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `no_negotiation`**
  - Yamlikha's firm rejection of compromise
  - "They want our souls, not our taxes" — refusal to worship idols
  - **Choice: refusing negotiation** maintains faith meter
  - Heart-strengthening shown through player agency

---

### **18:15-16** - *"These, our people, have taken besides Him deities... And when you have withdrawn from them and that which they worship other than God, retreat to the cave."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `start`, `gathering`, `fleeing_city`**
  - Idols explicitly present in ancient city environment
  - Emperor demands worship of false gods
  - Choice to flee rather than compromise
  - Cave as refuge from idolatry

---

### **18:17** - *"And you would see the sun when it rose, inclining away from their cave on the right, and when it set, passing away from them on the left, while they were [laying] within an open space thereof."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `entering_cave`**
  - Cave entrance shows dim sunlight
  - Environment design: cave opening visible, not completely enclosed
  - Lighting: warm sunlight filters in during entering/waking scenes

---

### **18:18** - *"And you would think them awake, while they were asleep. And We turned them to the right and to the left, while their dog stretched his forelegs at the entrance."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `divine_sleep`, `time_passage`**
  - Dog companion introduced before entering cave
  - Animation: Sleepers positioned in circle, dog in center
  - Visual note: Characters appear peaceful, not lifeless
  - **Gap filled:** Dog is now a persistent character, not just mentioned

---

### **18:19** - *"And similarly, We awakened them that they might question one another. Said a speaker from among them, 'How long have you remained [here]?' They said, 'We have remained a day or part of a day.' They said, 'Your Lord is most knowing of how long you remained. So send one of you with this silver coin of yours to the city and let him look to which is the best of food and bring you provision from it and let him be cautious. And let no one be aware of you.'"*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `waking`, `confusion_about_time`**
  - Sleepers wake and immediately debate duration
  - Different guesses: "A day, or part of a day" vs "No, longer"
  - Yamlikha: *"Your Lord knows best how long you have stayed"* (direct quote)
- **Scene: `volunteer_mission`, `yamlikha_mission`**
  - Choice: player or Yamlikha goes to city
  - Silver coins explicitly mentioned
  - Direct quote: *"Let him look for which food is purest, and let him bring you provision from it. And let him be careful, and let no one be aware of you."*
- **Scene: `currency_shock`, `revelation_of_time`**
  - Vendor recognizes coin as ancient (300 years old)
  - Currency scene is the **primary revelation mechanism**
  - **Gap filled:** Original version had this, but 3D makes it visceral and animated

---

### **18:20** - *"Indeed, if they come to know of you, they will stone you or return you to their religion. And never would you succeed, then - ever."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `volunteer_mission`, `yamlikha_mission`**
  - Warning: *"Let no one be aware of you"* before leaving
  - Fear of persecution still present (though now historical)
  - Irony: They feared violence, but find reverence instead

---

### **18:21** - *"And similarly, We caused them to be found that they [who found them] would know that the promise of God is true and that of the Hour there is no doubt. [That was] when they disputed among themselves about their affair and said, 'Construct over them a structure. Their Lord is most knowing about them.' Said those who prevailed in the matter, 'We will surely take [for ourselves] over them a masjid.'"*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `discovery_scene`, `crowd_follows`**
  - Townspeople discover the sleepers
  - Direct quote: *"Thus We made them known to the people, that they might know that the promise of God is true."*
  - Debates about what to build over the cave referenced in endings
  - **Gap filled:** Discovery is now a full scene with animation, not just narration

---

### **18:22** - *"They will say there were three, the fourth of them being their dog; and they will say there were five, the sixth of them being their dog - guessing at the unseen; and they will say there were seven, and the eighth of them was their dog. Say, [O Muhammad], 'My Lord is most knowing of their number. None knows them except a few. So do not argue about them except with an obvious argument and do not inquire about them among them from anyone.'"*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `ending_sign`**
  - Direct quote of the debate: *"How many were they? Some will say they were three, their dog the fourth. Some will say five, their dog the sixth, guessing at the unseen. Some will say seven, and their dog the eighth."* (18:22)
  - Followed by: *"Your Lord knows best concerning them."* (18:22)
  - **Gap filled:** Original version didn't include this meta-commentary

---

### **18:23-24** - *"And never say of anything, 'Indeed, I will do that tomorrow,' except [when adding], 'If God wills.'..."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Thematic integration:**
  - Faith mechanic: Player cannot control outcomes, only choices
  - Endings emphasize divine will over human agency
  - Time itself is shown to be in God's control

---

### **18:25** - *"And they remained in their cave for three hundred years and exceeded by nine."*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `time_passage`**
  - Direct quote: *"Three hundred years pass. Solar years."* (18:25)
  - Explicit duration given (not vague "centuries")
  - **Gap filled:** Original version said "three hundred years" but didn't cite verse

---

### **18:26** - *"Say, 'God is most knowing of how long they remained. To Him belongs the unseen [aspects] of the heavens and the earth. How Seeing is He and how Hearing! They have not besides Him any protector, and He shares not His legislation with anyone.'"*

**Game Implementation:**
- **Scene: `confusion_about_time`**
  - Yamlikha's line: *"Your Lord knows best how long you have stayed"*
- **Ending: All variations**
  - Emphasis that this is a divine act, not human achievement
  - Faith meter reflects trust in God's plan, not control

## What the Visual Novel Was Missing

The original 2D visual novel version had these **gaps** (now filled in 3D):

1. ❌ **No explicit prayer scene before fleeing** → ✅ Now `prayer_before_flight` scene
2. ❌ **Divine sealing not mentioned** → ✅ Now explicit in `divine_sleep`
3. ❌ **Time debate vague** → ✅ Now central to `confusion_about_time`
4. ❌ **Currency scene understated** → ✅ Now animated in `currency_shock`
5. ❌ **Discovery not dramatized** → ✅ Now full scene with crowd animation
6. ❌ **Debate about number not included** → ✅ Now in `ending_sign`
7. ❌ **300 years not sourced to verse** → ✅ Now cited as (18:25)
8. ❌ **Dog mentioned once, then forgotten** → ✅ Now persistent through cave scenes

## Why This Matters

The Quranic story of the Cave Sleepers is not just about:
- Miraculous sleep ❌
- Time travel ❌
- Ancient legends ❌

It's about:
- **Refuge from tyranny** ✅
- **Divine preservation of faith** ✅
- **God's power over time** ✅
- **Being a sign for future generations** ✅

Every scene in this game is designed to emphasize these themes while staying true to the verses.
